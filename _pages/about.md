<p style="text-align: justify;"> 
    I am a research assistant at <a href="https://www.star-iitb.in/" target="_blank">STAR Lab</a>, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB), working with <a href="https://www.star-iitb.in/group/varun-bhalerao" target="_blank">Prof. Varun Bhalerao</a>. I hold a Master's in Physics (Astronomy Minor) and have a strong interest in studying compact objects like <strong>Neutron Stars</strong> and <strong>Black Holes</strong>. My current research focuses on <strong>accretion physics</strong> in <strong>X-ray Binary systems</strong>. Additionally, I study <strong>transient astrophysical events</strong> such as <strong>Gamma-ray Bursts (GRBs)</strong> and <strong>Electromagnetic counterparts of Gravitational Wave events</strong>. I use data from leading observatories, including <a href="https://astrosat.iucaa.in/" target="_blank">AstroSat</a>, <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/growthindia/" target="_blank">GROWTH-India Telescope</a>, and the <a href="https://www.ztf.caltech.edu/" target="_blank">Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF)</a>.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
    Beyond research, I am deeply involved in <strong>science communication</strong>. I lead a team that organizes <strong>asteroid search campaigns</strong>, a citizen science initiative at the <a href="https://www.sserd.org" target="_blank">Society for Space Education Research and Development (SSERD)</a>, in collaboration with the <a href="http://iasc.cosmosearch.org/" target="_blank">International Asteroid Search Campaign (IASC)</a> and NASA.
</p>


<p style="text-align: justify;">
    Outside of science, I am a percussionist and vocalist trained in <strong>Indian Classical Music</strong>.
</p>
