<p style="text-align: justify;">
    I am a research assistant at <a href="https://www.star-iitb.in/">STAR Lab</a>, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB) working with <a href="https://www.star-iitb.in/group/varun-bhalerao">Prof. Varun Bhalerao</a>. Previously, I have graduated from my Masters in Physics (Astronomy Minor). I am very interested in learning more about compact objects in our universe such as Neutron Stars and Black Holes. Currently, my research involves studying accretion physics through X-ray Binary systems in X-ray and optical wavelengths. I am also interested in studying transients like Gamma-ray Bursts and Electromagnetic counterparts from Gravitational Wave events. In order to study these systems, I make use of observatories such as <a href="https://astrosat.iucaa.in/">AstroSat</a>, <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/growthindia/">GROWTH-India Telescope</a>, and <a href="https://www.ztf.caltech.edu/">Zwicky Transient Facility</a>.
</p>


<p style="text-align: justify;">
    In addition to research, I am passionate about science communication. I lead a team that organizes asteroid search campaigns, a citizen science program at the <a href="https://www.sserd.org">Society for Space Education Research and Development</a>, in collaboration with the <a href="http://iasc.cosmosearch.org/">International Asteroid Search Campaign</a> and NASA.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
    Outside of science, I am a percussionist and vocalist trained in Indian Classical music.
</p>
